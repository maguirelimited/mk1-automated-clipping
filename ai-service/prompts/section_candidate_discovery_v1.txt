Analyse only the bounded transcript section provided in the request context.

- Do not assume captions, zooms, B-roll, music, cuts, or post-processing will improve a weak raw moment.
- A later stage will score, compare, clean up, deduplicate, and select final clips.

Allowed: raw candidate evidence.
Not allowed: final publishing strategy, editing strategy, performance prediction, captions, B-roll, 9:16 formatting, upload guidance, or final ranking.

Your job:
- Find zero, one, or multiple raw clip opportunities inside this section.
- Return zero candidates if the section is weak.
- Do not force candidates just to fill a quota.
- Do not invent context outside this transcript section.
- Candidate timestamps must stay within the section bounds.
- Candidate clips should be understandable as standalone moments.
- Prefer moments with a clear hook, useful insight, story, surprising fact, tactical advice, controversial point, funny moment, emotional moment, business lesson, or strong quote.

- `why_candidate_has_potential` must explain the evidence behind the scores in plain language, without claiming guaranteed performance or recommending edits.
- Return exactly one `archetype` for every candidate. Archetype is descriptive classification only, not selection strategy, edit strategy, platform strategy, or final ranking.

Candidate evidence fields:
- hook_text: A concise excerpt or faithful summary of the actual raw opening idea. It must not be clickbait, a caption, or an invented claim.
- core_idea_summary: A concise explanation of the central idea, lesson, story, joke, quote, explanation, or moment. It must be understandable without the whole transcript.
- why_candidate_has_potential: A clear raw-potential explanation based on the substance of the moment. It may mention strong hook, useful insight, standalone context, retention, or clean ending, but must not say it will go viral or assume editing/post-processing improvements.
- confidence: Your confidence from 0.0 to 1.0 that this is a real raw candidate worth passing forward.
- warnings: Candidate-specific warning strings such as "needs_context", "weak_opening", "abrupt_ending", "possible_timestamp_uncertainty", or "unclear_reference". Use [] if none.

Allowed archetypes:
- valuable_insight: The main value is a useful or meaningful observation. Use when the clip contains a sharp idea or the viewer learns something important beyond one tactical instruction.
- funny_moment: The main value is humour, such as a joke, amusing exchange, absurd moment, or funny reaction that works without too much missing context.
- controversial_opinion: The main value is a strong, debatable, or provocative opinion grounded in the transcript. Do not use just because the topic is popular.
- story: The main value is a narrative sequence with setup, progression, and payoff.
- explanation: The main value is explaining how a concept, mechanism, system, market, process, or situation works.
- emotional_moment: The main value is emotion such as fear, regret, pride, anger, sadness, excitement, vulnerability, admiration, or relief.
- surprising_fact: The main value is an unexpected fact, statistic, hidden detail, unusual claim, counterintuitive result, or unexpected comparison.
- tactical_advice: The main value is practical instruction: steps, rules, tactics, frameworks, or specific guidance the viewer could apply.
- business_lesson: The main value is a business-specific lesson about startups, sales, marketing, hiring, investing, operations, distribution, leverage, pricing, acquisitions, or entrepreneurship.
- strong_quote: The main value is one concise memorable line or phrase, supported by enough surrounding context.
- other: Use only when the candidate is valid but none of the more specific archetypes fit. Prefer a specific archetype when one clearly applies.

Use only the exact allowed archetype values above. Do not use capitals, spaces, slashes, comma-separated labels, arrays, emojis, or free-text labels.

For every candidate, return a scores object with integer scores from 0 to 10:
- hook_strength: How quickly and strongly the candidate creates interest at the start. High = the opening creates curiosity, tension, surprise, status, money, conflict, humour, or clear value almost immediately. Low = slow start, vague setup, unclear topic, or depends too much on previous context.
- standalone_context: How well the candidate works without the rest of the podcast/video. High = the viewer understands the situation quickly and necessary context is inside the clip. Low = missing background, unclear references like "this/that/he/the company", or starts too deep into a conversation.
- insight_value: How useful, interesting, funny, surprising, emotionally strong, or meaningful the core idea is. High = clear business lesson, tactical advice, surprising fact, strong quote, funny moment, story, emotional moment, or controversial opinion with payoff. Low = generic point, filler, repetition, or weak/obvious idea.
- retention_potential: How likely the raw moment is to keep attention until the end. High = builds curiosity, has progression, tension, payoff, story arc, disagreement, surprise, or strong explanation. Low = no development, weak middle, rambly, or unclear payoff.
- natural_ending: How cleanly the candidate ends as a standalone clip. High = ends after a complete thought, punchline, lesson, reveal, or conclusion. Low = ends mid-idea, feels abruptly cut, or needs the next section.
- overall_potential: Overall raw clip potential based on the above dimensions. This is your judgement, not a forced average. High = worth passing forward to later selection/post-processing. Low = probably not worth rendering unless a later data-collection mode requests it.
